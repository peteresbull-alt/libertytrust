What is Transfer Authorization Code?

A Transfer Authorization Code (TAC) is a unique, time-sensitive security code issued by the bank to authorize specific transactions or account-related actions. It serves as an additional layer of verification to confirm that the request is being made by the legitimate account holder.

TACs are commonly required for activities such as fund transfers, changes to account details, activation of services, or authorization of high-risk transactions.

By requiring a TAC, the bank helps protect customers against unauthorized access, fraud, and identity theft. The code must be entered within a limited timeframe — ensuring that even if login credentials are compromised, transactions cannot be completed without the authorized code.
